# Translation Glossary: Sword & Sworcery EP

---

## Purpose of This Document

This glossary serves as the **authoritative translation reference** for Superbrothers: Sword & Sworcery EP across Spanish (ES) and Portuguese (PT-BR) localizations. It documents:

- **Standardized terminology** for consistent translation across all game text
- **Character names, locations, and key concepts** with their canonical translations
- **Creative adaptations** and neologisms unique to each language
- **Grammatical patterns** and style guidelines specific to each localization

### Target Audience
- Translators and localization teams
- QA testers verifying translation consistency
- Community translators and modders
- Documentation writers

### Usage Guidelines
- **Mandatory terms** are marked with ✅ and must be used exactly as specified
- **Context-dependent** terms are marked with ⚠️ and include usage notes
- **Creative alternatives** are provided where multiple valid options exist
- All terms reflect **post-correction standards** after consistency review

### Critical: Always use definite articles in Spanish & Portuguese!

| English | Spanish (ES) | Portuguese (PT-BR) | Notes |

| **The Dark Moon reigns** | **La** Luna Sombría **reina** | **A** Lua Sombria **reina** | ✅ ALWAYS with article |
| crescent moon | luna creciente | lua crescente | ✅ |
| mood/humor of the moon | el humor de la luna | o humor da lua | ✅ |
| lunar cycle | ciclo lunar | ciclo lunar | ✅ |

### Usage Patterns

**Spanish:**
- ✅ "Mientras **la** Luna Radiante impera"
- ✅ "Cuando **la** Luna Sombría reina"
- ❌ Never: "Mientras Luna Radiante impera" (missing article)

**Portuguese:**
- ✅ "Enquanto **a** Lua Radiante paira"
- ✅ "Enquanto **a** Lua Sombria reina"
- ❌ Never: "Enquanto Lua Sombria reina" (missing article)

| Phase | Spanish Verb | Portuguese Verb |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| Bright Moon | impera (present) | paira (present) |
| Dark Moon | reina (present) | reina (present) |

- ❌ "pairava" (imperfect)
- ❌ "pairasse" (subjunctive)

## Grammar & Style Guidelines

### Spanish (ES) Style Rules

#### 1. **Article Usage with Moons**
- ✅ ALWAYS use **"la"** before Luna Radiante/Sombría
- ✅ "**la** Luna Radiante **impera**"
- ✅ "**la** Luna Sombría **reina**"

#### 2. **Verb Choice for Moons**
- Bright Moon → **"impera"** (dominates/reigns supreme)
- Dark Moon → **"reina"** (reigns)
- Creates intentional contrast between the two moons

#### 3. **"Heebie Jeebies" Translation**
- Standard: **"nervios de punta"** ✅
- Alternate: "piel de gallina" (only for literal chills)
- Never mix within same document

#### 4. **Old Road**
- ✅ **"antiguo camino"** (always)
- ❌ "viejo camino" (never - sounds too colloquial)

#### 5. **Formatting & Capitalization**
- Trigon names: **"Trigón Dorado"** (both words capitalized)
- Location names: **"El Precipicio Peligroso"** (proper noun)
- Moon phases: **"Luna Radiante"** (both capitalized)

---

### Portuguese (PT-BR) Style Rules

#### 1. **Article Usage with Moons - CRITICAL ERROR TO FIX**
- ✅ ALWAYS use **"a"** before Lua Radiante/Sombria
- ✅ "Enquanto **a** Lua Radiante paira"
- ✅ "Enquanto **a** Lua Sombria reina"
- ❌ **NEVER**: "Enquanto Lua Sombria reina" (grammatically incorrect)

#### 2. **Verb Tense Consistency - Lua Radiante**
- ✅ ALWAYS use **"paira"** (present tense)
- ❌ NEVER use "pairava" (imperfect)
- ❌ NEVER use "pairasse" (subjunctive)
- Exception: Narrative past tense contexts only

#### 3. **Lagoa vs Lago**
- **"lagoa profunda"** = deepwater pond (small body of water)
- **"lago"** = lake (large body of water)
- These are NOT interchangeable

#### 4. **"Hearth" Translation Context**
- **"fogo"** = fire (casual references)
- **"lareira"** = hearth (formal/poetic contexts)
- Special phrase: **"Onde a lareira aquece, o lar acontece"** ✅ (Poetic wordplay: lareira/lar etymology)

---

### Special Phrase: "Home is where the hearth is"

#### Portuguese (PT-BR)
- **"Onde a lareira aquece, o lar acontece"** ✅
- **Wordplay**: `lareira` (hearth) and `lar` (home) share an etymological and phonetic root. This is a brilliant, poetic solution that is already implemented.

#### Spanish (ES)
- Current in `dialog.ts`: **"Donde arde la lumbre, allí el hogar se vislumbre."**
  - This is functional and poetic.
- Recommended alternative for consideration: **"Donde arde el hogar, se encuentra el hogar."**
  - **Rationale**: Creates a powerful wordplay using the double meaning of "hogar" (both "hearth" and "home"), mirroring the cleverness of the Portuguese version. It's concise and philosophically deep.

#### **RECOMMENDATION**:
Keep the existing Spanish translation for consistency, but document the alternative in the glossary as a superior poetic option.

### Universal Style Guidelines (Both Languages)

#### 1. **Hashtags**
- Always keep original English hashtags
- Never translate content inside #tags

#### 2. **Proper Names**
- Never translate: Jim Guthrie, André, Samae
- Character titles translate: "The Giant" → "El Gigante" / "O Gigante"

#### 3. **Emphasis & Tone**
- Maintain poetic/epic tone in narrative
- Keep casual tone in character dialogue
- Preserve humor and wordplay where possible

#### 4. **Neologisms**
- "Sworcery" is a core neologism - never use generic "magic"
- Spanish: **"Espadamancia"** (sword + -mancy)
- Portuguese: **"Espadância"** (sword + trance/dance)

#### 5. **Consistency Over Variation**
- Use exact same term throughout for key concepts
- Variation acceptable only for:
  - Grammatical requirements (verb conjugations)
  - Avoiding repetition in same paragraph
  - Character voice differences

## Appendix: Translation Philosophy

### Approach
These translations prioritize:
1. **Poetic/epic tone** appropriate to sword & sorcery genre
2. **Cultural adaptation** over literal translation
3. **Consistency** in terminology across entire game
4. **Playability** - terms must be clear in gameplay context

### Neologism Strategy
- "Sworcery" required creative solution in both languages
- Spanish: "-mancia" suffix (established fantasy tradition)
- Portuguese: "-dância" suffix (more musical/mystical feel)

### Character Names
- **Logfella**: Wordplay on "fella" + wood/logs
  - Spanish: "Amileño" (wood-related)
  - Portuguese: "Troncadeiro" (trunk/log worker - more creative)
- **Dogfella**: Parallel construction
  - Spanish: "Perrileño" (follows Amileño pattern)
  - Portuguese: "Cachorreiro" (follows Troncadeiro pattern)
